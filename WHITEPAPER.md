## I. The Vanguard Backbone (Progressive Semantic Abstraction)
**The Problem:** Standard networks often apply complex attention or routing mechanisms directly to early layers. This is computationally reckless. Raw pixels have high spatial dimensionality but incredibly low individual semantic value (knowing one pixel is "red" tells the router nothing).

**The Solution:** The Vanguard Block is a deep, multi-stage convolutional hierarchy (`Conv -> SpLR_V2 -> MaxPool`). It violently compresses the spatial dimensions ($H \times W$) while expanding the feature depth ($C$). 
* By the time the tensor reaches the core Highway, the network is no longer routing "pixels." It is routing highly concentrated semantic concepts (e.g., *fur texture* or *wheel shape*). This is **Latent Stage Routing**, maximizing computational economy.

## II. The Information Blinder & The Symmetry Breaking Problem
**The Problem:** When you create a Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) layer with parallel heads, they suffer from the "Symmetry Breaking Problem." If Head A finds an easy feature, Head B and C will copy Head A to minimize loss. The network becomes redundant.

**The Solution:** The network utilizes an **Information Blinder** to physically segregate the domains. The three MoE paths (The Primary, The Shadow Mutator, and The Ghost Highway) are mathematically forced to look at completely different frequencies:
* **The Anchor (Primary):** Processes standard spatial relationships.
* **The Sniper (Shadow Mutator):** Processes pure mathematical residual error ($X - Anchor$). It is blind to what the Anchor already knows.
* **The Ghost (Global):** Uses alternating dilated convolutions to process wide-angle, macro-level context. 

By amputating their shared vision, the architecture forces them to become absolute specialists.

## III. The 70/30 Elastic Router (Exploitation vs. Exploration)
**The Problem:** In standard MoE architectures, if one expert becomes slightly better than the others, the router sends all traffic to it. The "underdog" experts starve to death, their weights stagnate, and the network suffers from Mode Collapse.

**The Solution:** MACRO-DREADNOUGHT utilizes a mathematically bounded 70/30 Elastic Router. 
* 70% of the traffic is dynamically allocated based on the layer's real-time confidence (Exploitation).
* 30% of the traffic is forced into a uniform distribution (Exploration). 

This guarantees that the underdog heads never starve. They are kept on life support, constantly training on background data, waiting for the Alpha head to fail so they can immediately take over.
